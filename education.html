Personal Carbon Summary

1. What is a carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases, especially carbon dioxide (CO₂), produced by a person's daily activities. This can come from transportation, food choices, electricity use, and other lifestyle habits.

For example, driving a car, eating a beef-heavy meal, or using a lot of electricity can all increase a person's carbon footprint.

2. Why does carbon footprint matter?

Carbon footprint matters because greenhouse gas emissions contribute to climate change. Climate change can lead to stronger heat waves, flooding, droughts, rising sea levels, and harm to ecosystems.

Understanding carbon footprint helps people connect everyday actions with environmental impact. Even small changes can make a difference when many people do them consistently.

3. Simple emission calculation logic

Simple idea: Emissions = Activity Amount × Emission Factor

This means we can estimate emissions by taking how much of an activity someone does and multiplying it by a simple factor.

  • Transportation: distance × factor for the travel type
  • Food: number of meals × factor for the meal type
  • Electricity: amount of energy used × factor for electricity

4. Simple environmental impact examples

  • Driving alone usually creates more emissions than taking a bus.
  • Walking or biking usually creates little to no direct carbon emissions.
  • Beef-heavy meals usually create more emissions than vegetarian meals.
  • Using more electricity at home generally increases household emissions.

If a person replaces short car trips with walking a few times each week, their total carbon footprint can decrease over time.
