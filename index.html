Education

Understanding Carbon Footprint

Learn what carbon footprint means, why it matters, and how simple daily choices affect the environment.

1. What is a carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases, especially CO₂, produced by a person’s daily activities such as transportation, food, and electricity use.

2. Why does it matter?

Carbon emissions contribute to climate change, which leads to extreme weather, rising sea levels, and environmental damage. Understanding your footprint helps connect everyday choices with global impact.

3. Simple emission calculation logic

Emissions = Activity × Emission Factor

  • Transport: distance × factor
  • Food: meals × factor
  • Energy: electricity × factor

This is a simplified model to help compare choices.

4. Environmental impact examples

  • Driving produces more emissions than public transport
  • Walking or biking produces almost zero emissions
  • Beef meals have higher emissions than vegetarian meals
  • More electricity use increases carbon footprint
