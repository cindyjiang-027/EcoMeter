Quick Tips

Start with the highest-leverage changes

Small changes are not all equally useful. The goal is to notice which repeated behaviors create the most impact, and start there.

🚗

Transport Tip

Click to expand

Instead of asking how to eliminate driving completely, ask which trips do not actually require a car. Short and repeated trips are usually the easiest place to reduce emissions without changing your whole routine.
🥗

Food Tip

Click to expand

Not all meals matter equally. Beef-heavy meals usually dominate food emissions, so reducing the highest-impact category often gives more benefit than trying to change everything at once.
⚡

Energy Tip

Click to expand

Energy use is easy to ignore because it feels invisible. The bigger issue is usually not one-time use, but the baseline habits that happen every day, such as cooling, heating, lighting, and always-on devices.

Concept

What is a carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint is a way to estimate how much greenhouse gas impact is created by everyday activities such as transportation, food, and energy use.

What makes it useful is that it turns invisible systems into something comparable. Driving, eating, and using electricity may feel like unrelated actions, but carbon footprint gives them a shared unit so people can understand which choices matter more.

Logic

How the calculation works

Basic idea

Emissions = Activity Amount × Emission Factor

Examples

Transport distance × average emissions per km
Food meal type × average emissions per meal
Energy electricity use × emissions per kWh
